# system_portfolio
University project

Problem Statement

Students at National Louis University are struggling with course registration and navigating the current registration system.

The process creates confusion, stress, and uncertainty — especially for first-year, international, transfer, and non-linear pathway students.

Evidence Analysis Summary

Based on collected data and informal interviews:

A majority of students reported feeling confused or overwhelmed during course registration.

Many students were unsure which courses were required for their major or degree progress.

Students frequently mentioned technical issues such as:

System lag

Registration errors

Courses appearing full unexpectedly

Satisfaction Data

33.3% of students reported being satisfied with D2L registration

66.7% reported being unsatisfied

This indicates a clear system-level usability and clarity problem.

Insights from Conversations / Informal Interviews

Several consistent themes emerged:

Students rely on friends or group chats instead of official university systems.

Academic advisors are difficult to access during peak registration periods.

First-year and international students experience significantly higher levels of confusion.

Transfer students, double majors, and students changing programs face additional complexity navigating D2L.

This suggests the current system does not adequately support diverse academic pathways.

How Might We Statements
1️⃣ How might we reduce the cognitive load students experience while navigating multiple disconnected systems during course registration?

Evidence Basis:
Students must switch between advising documents, degree audits, emails, and registration tools to understand what to do.

2️⃣ How might we help students clearly understand course requirements and prerequisite sequencing?

Evidence Basis:
Students frequently discover prerequisite issues or sequencing conflicts too late in the process.

3️⃣ How might we support students who do not follow a standard academic path in navigating registration independently?

Evidence Basis:
Transfer students, double majors, and students changing programs report higher levels of confusion.

Solution Directions

To address these problems, we propose:

Adding an integrated planning view within D2L

Creating clear prerequisite visibility and sequencing pathways

Developing an early conflict warning system to prevent registration errors

Providing a visual academic roadmap aligned with degree requirements

These solutions aim to reduce stress, increase independence, and improve registration confidence.
